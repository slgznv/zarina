Lesson 11

Reading

Task №1: Fill in the blanks with appropriate words from the word bank: (discipline, challenges, success, difficult, motivation)


"If you want to achieve ____, you can’t rely on ____. Success comes from ________, which is built by doing the hard things every single day. Many people give up because they think the path is too ______, but the truth is that the obstacles are what make you stronger. Instead of avoiding _______, embrace them and grow."

Task №2: Summary


Write a 3-sentence summary of this passage. What is Goggins' main point?

Task №3: Critical Thinking


Why does Goggins believe obstacles are essential for growth? Write 3-4 sentences explaining your thoughts with examples from your own life.

Writing

Task №1: Essay "Motivation or Discipline?"


Write a 200-250 word essay answering

•Which is more important for success: motivation or discipline?

•Use examples from Goggins’ podcast to support your argument.

Task №2: Podcast Script


Write a short script sharing a personal story of overcoming difficulties.

Task №3: Goal Setting Plan


Create a detailed plan for achieving a long-term goal.

Speaking

Task №1:Role Play


One student plays David Goggins, and the other acts as the interviewer. Below are example questions the "interviewer" can ask:

• What was the biggest obstacle you faced in your life, and how did you overcome it?

• Can you describe a moment when you almost gave up but chose to push through?

• What do you believe is the key to achieving success?

• Why do you think discipline is more reliable than motivation?

Task №2: Debate


Partner up or record yourself answering these prompts: Topic: “Discipline is more important than motivation for achieving success.”Split into two groups, with one side supporting the statement and the other opposing it.

Task №3:Motivational Speech


Prepare a 2-minute motivational speech on overcoming fears or challenges. Use analogies and impactful phrases to inspire your audience, just as Goggins does.

Listening

Task №1:Analyzing Key Ideas


•Listen to the first 5 minutes and identify three main ideas discussed by David Goggins.

•Explain how each idea connects to his overall message about discipline and resilience.

Task №2: Critical Perception


Identify where in Goggins' speech he appeals to the emotions of his listeners. How does he do this? What words or phrases emphasize this?

Task №3:Multiple Choice Questions


After listening to a segment, answer comprehension questions like:

1.What does David say is necessary to build resilience?
a) Avoiding failure

b) Facing challenges head-on

c) Relying on others' opinions

2.Why does David emphasize discipline over motivation?

a) Motivation is unpredictable and temporary.

b) Discipline requires less effort.

c) Motivation comes from external sources.

3.What metaphor does David use to describe the process of growth?

a) A smooth road to success

b) A mountain that gets steeper with every step

c) A blade sharpened by pressure

4.What is David's view on failure?

a) It’s a sign you should stop trying.

b) It’s an opportunity to learn and grow.

c) It’s something to avoid at all costs.

5.According to David, how should we deal with obstacles?

a) Ignore them and focus on the positives.

b) Use them as tools for growth and improvement.

c) Wait until the obstacles resolve themselves.
