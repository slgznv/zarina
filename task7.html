Lesson 7

Reading

Task №1: Comprehension Questions (Active Voice Focus)


Answer the following questions in complete sentences, making sure to use the active voice:

• Why do people need to improve themselves constantly?

• How does learning new skills make people more independent?

• What are the benefits of learning new skills for your mental health?

• How does learning a new language help in your career?

• Why do employers value people who learn new things?

Task №2: Find the Mistake:


In the following sentences taken from the text, one of them incorrectly uses the passive voice. Find the mistake and rewrite the sentence in active voice:

•"Learning new skills is valued by employers."

•"People are constantly challenged by learning."

•"Studies show that learning improves mental health."

Task №3: Summary Challenge


Write a summary of the text in 5-6 sentences using only the active voice. Focus on the key points, such as the benefits of learning new skills for work, health, and confidence.

Writing

Task №1: Rewrite Passive Sentences into Active Voice


Here are three sentences written in the passive voice. Rewrite each one in the active voice:

•"New skills are acquired by people to stay competitive."

•"Languages are learned by students to improve job prospects."

•"Healthy habits are developed by travelers during their trips."

Task №2: Personal Reflection Essay (200 words, Active Voice Required):


Write an essay on the following topic: How I Plan to Learn New Skills in the Next Year. Use active voice to explain:

What specific skills you plan to learn.

How you will go about learning them.

How these skills will benefit your future.

Task №3: Skill Development Story:


Write a story (150-200 words) about someone who learned a difficult skill. Use the active voice to describe how this person took action, faced challenges, and succeeded.

Speaking

Task №1: Debate Task


Engage in a discussion or record yourself answering this prompt: Is it more important to specialize in one skill or to learn many different skills? Use active voice in your arguments, e.g., "I believe learning many skills helps people adapt faster to change."

Task №2: Role Play and Discussion


Partner up or record yourself answering these prompts:

•Imagine you are at a job interview. The interviewer asks: “Tell me about a new skill you learned recently and how it has helped you.”

•Use the active voice to explain how the skill improved your performance or abilities.

Task №3: Giving Advice


Imagine a friend asks you for advice on how to learn a new language. Use active voice to explain the steps they should take. Example: "First, you should find a good teacher. Then, practice speaking every day. Finally, immerse yourself in the language by watching shows and reading."

Listening

Task №1:Podcast Reflection


After listening, write down three sentences the speaker says using active voice. Then, paraphrase each sentence, keeping it in active voice.

•Example: "The speaker said, 'I learned to code last year.' My paraphrase: 'Last year, I picked up coding skills.'

Task №2: Record Yourself Giving a Summary


Listen to a short talk or video about personal development. Record yourself summarizing it in active voice. Use sentences like: "The speaker explained how we can improve our time management." Practice using clear, active sentences.

Task №3: Active Voice vs. Passive Voice Identification


Identify 3 sentences in active voice and 3 in passive voice. Write both sets of sentences and then rewrite the passive sentences into active voice.

Example Passive: "This technique is used by many people."

Example Active: "Many people use this technique."
