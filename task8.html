Lesson 8

Reading

Task №1: Inferential Comprehension:


Read the text carefully and answer the following questions, which require critical thinking and analysis. Support your answers with examples from the text.

•Why might learning a foreign language be considered enriching?

• What cultural aspects mentioned in the text help students understand the language better?

• Why might some students feel "stronger" after mastering a foreign language?

Task №2: Identify Present Perfect Use and Purpose:


Find all the sentences in the text that use the Present Perfect tense and identify the purpose of each one (e.g., expressing completed actions, indefinite past, recent events, or personal experiences).

Task №3:Critical Thinking - Evaluate Perspectives:


Evaluate how applicable the different language learning methods in the text are for various types of learners. Write a short analysis explaining which approaches are most effective for achieving fluency, giving 2–3 examples using the Present Perfect to describe your reasoning.

Writing

Task №1: Analytical Essay with Personal Experience:


Write an analytical essay (200-250 words) on the topic, "Why Perseverance is Key in Language Learning." In your essay:

•Explain why persistence is helpful in learning a language.

•Use examples from the text and your own experience (e.g., "I have overcome challenges...") and include at least 5 sentences in the Present Perfect.

Task №2: Personal Reflection::


Write a short text about your own experience of learning a language (English or Chinese) using Present Perfect. Answer the questions:

•What achievements do you have so far?

•What difficulties have you had to overcome?

•How do you plan to improve your skills?

Task №3: Formal Letter of Advice:


Write a formal letter giving advice to a student who is beginning to study a new language. Provide 3–4 recommendations based on your own or others' experiences, using the Present Perfect (e.g., "Many students have found...").

Speaking

Task №1: Debate:


In pairs or groups, discuss the question: "Is Cultural Immersion Essential for Language Fluency?" Justify your opinion and share your experiences (real or imagined) in the Present Perfect (e.g., "I have experienced...").

Task №2: Advanced Presentation:


Prepare a 5-minute presentation on "The Most Effective Language Learning Methods," where you:

•Discuss different approaches (e.g., immersion, grammar-focused learning

•Share your experiences using at least 5 sentences in the Present Perfect to describe achievements or challenges you've encountered in language learning.

Task №3: Role-Play with Critical Feedback:


In pairs, role-play a situation where one of you is a teacher and the other is a student who has been studying a language for 5 years but faces challenges. The teacher uses Present Perfect to give advice and discuss how to improve (e.g., "You have already practiced...").

Listening

Task №1:Comprehension Questions


Answer the following questions based on your understanding of the main ideas and examples.

1.According to the speakers, what is one common misconception about practicing effectively?

2.How does the brain benefit from frequent, shorter practice sessions compared to longer, infrequent ones?

3.Describe one technique that Bosler and Greene suggest to help learners stay focused during practice sessions.

Task №2: Gap-Fill Exercise


Listen to the podcast again and fill in the blanks in the following sentences with words or phrases from the audio.

1."Research has shown that the brain is more likely to retain information when practice is ______ over time."

2."One of the best ways to build new skills is to practice ______, focusing on smaller parts of the skill rather than the whole."

3."The speakers emphasize that having a clear goal in mind during practice can ______ our ability to improve."

Task №3:Summary and Reflection


After listening to the podcast, summarize the main tips Bosler and Greene provide for effective practice. Use the Present Perfect to describe how these techniques have proven effective in helping people improve. For example: "Studies have shown that distributed practice helps learners retain skills better."

Prompts for your summary:

•What are two or three key strategies the speakers discuss?

• How have these strategies been effective, according to the speakers?

•Reflect briefly on your own experiences with practice and mention any techniques from the podcast that you might use in the future.
