Lesson 14

Reading

Task №1: Comprehension Check


Read the text and answer the following questions. Use the First Conditional in your answers.

• If you set measurable goals, what will happen to your progress over time?

• What will happen if you don’t prioritize tasks effectively?

• If you take small, consistent steps toward your goals, what will you achieve?

Task №2: True or False Statements


Fill in the blanks in the following sentences with the correct form of the verb in the First Conditional.

•If you ___________ (set) clear goals, you ___________ (achieve) success faster.

• If you ___________ (fail) to plan your day, you ___________ (feel) overwhelmed.

• If you ___________ (visualize) your success, you ___________ (remain) motivated.

Task №3:Vocabulary in Context


Read the text and determine whether the following statements are true or false. Then, explain why, using the First Conditional.

If you set goals, you will never face any obstacles.

Setting realistic goals will make your progress slower.

Writing

Task №1: Essay


Write a short essay (150-200 words) on how setting goals can influence your personal or academic life. Use the First Conditional at least three times in your essay.

Instructions:

• ntroduce the topic of goal setting.

•Discuss the positive effects of setting goals and the importance of planning.

•Use the First Conditional to show what will happen if you take certain actions (e.g., setting deadlines or visualizing success).

•Conclude by emphasizing the long-term impact of goal setting on personal growth.

Task №2: Goal-Setting Plan:


Write a plan for achieving a personal goal, using the First Conditional to explain the steps involved.

Instructions:

•Choose a personal goal (e.g., learning a new language, getting fit, etc.).

•Break the goal down into smaller steps, using the First Conditional to describe what will happen if you follow each step (e.g., "If I practice every day, I will improve my skills").

•Set a deadline for each step and explain how you will stay motivated.

Task №3: Letter to a Friend:


Write a letter to a friend advising them on how to set and achieve their goals in the upcoming year. Use the First Conditional to offer suggestions on what will happen if they follow your advice.

Speaking

Task №1: Goal-Setting Advice:


magine you are giving a speech about the importance of goal setting. Use the First Conditional to explain how setting goals can lead to success.

Task №2: Role Play:


Pair up with a classmate. One of you is a coach, and the other is a person struggling to achieve their goals. The coach should offer advice using the First Conditional.

Task №3: Problem Solving with First Conditional:


Discuss with a partner what you would do if you faced setbacks in achieving your goals. Use the First Conditional to explain your solutions.

Instructions:

•Think of a scenario where your goals could be affected by obstacles (e.g., lack of time, motivation, etc.).

•Use the First Conditional to explain what actions you would take (e.g., "If I lose motivation, I will take a short break and remind myself of my goals").

•Discuss potential solutions to common problems in goal setting.

Listening

Task №1:Gap-Fill Exercise


Listen to the text and fill in the blanks with the missing words:

1."Setting goals is like creating a ________ for your life."

2."If you want to improve your skills in a foreign language, setting ________ or weekly targets will keep you motivated."

3."If you face ________, you will need to adjust your plans instead of giving up."

Task №2: True or False with Justification


Listen carefully to the text and decide whether the following statements are true or false. Justify your answers based on the recording.

If you don’t set clear goals, you will still use your time effectively.

Visualizing your success can help you stay motivated.

Facing challenges always means you should change your goals completely.

Task №3:


After listening to the text, predict what the speaker would say next in the following scenarios. Use the First Conditional in your answers.

•If someone spends too much time doubting themselves, what will happen to their progress?

•What will happen if you consistently take action toward your goals?

•If a person doesn’t visualize their success, how will it affect their motivation?
