Lesson 15

Reading

Task №1: Detailed Comprehension:


Identify three examples from the text where the author argues that creativity can solve practical problems. Explain how the second conditional is used to express these ideas.

Task №2: Critical Thinking:


Why does the author believe that fear of failure limits creativity? Use evidence from the text to support your answer and analyze how this connects to the use of second conditional sentences.

Task №3:Vocabulary in Context


If society prioritized imagination, as suggested in the text, what challenges might arise? Find evidence in the text and write a brief paragraph summarizing your view.

Writing

Task №1: Essay


Write an essay on: "If education systems prioritized creativity, what would the impact be on future generations?"

Use at least five second conditional sentences to describe hypothetical situations and their outcomes.

Task №2: Creative Writing:


Imagine you are an inventor. Write a short story about how the world would change if your invention were widely adopted. Include second conditional sentences to describe the hypothetical scenarios.

Task №3: Reflective Task:


Write a personal reflection on this question: "If I weren’t afraid of failure, what risks would I take to achieve my dreams?" Include at least four second conditional sentences.

Speaking

Task №1: Presentation:


Prepare and deliver a 3-4 minute speech on the topic: "How would your life change if imagination and creativity were your main priorities?"

In your speech, make sure to:

•Discuss how you currently use creativity in your daily life.

•Use at least three second conditional sentences to describe hypothetical changes or improvements in your life.

•Explain how imagination could help you solve a specific problem or achieve a personal goal.

•Share your opinion on whether creativity is valued enough in today’s society and support it with examples.

Task №2: Role Play:


Work with a partner. One of you is an educator, and the other is a student. Discuss: "If creativity were the focus of education, how would schools look different?"

Use the second conditional to suggest changes (e.g., teaching methods, classroom designs) and their possible results.

Task №3: Problem Solving with First Conditional:


Discuss this question with a group or partner: "If people weren’t afraid of failure, how would this change innovation in fields like technology or art?"

Share personal opinions and examples while incorporating at least two second conditional sentences for each idea.

Listening

Task №1:Gap-Fill Exercise


Listen to the text and fill in the blanks with the missing words:

1."If people used their imagination more frequently, they would discover new ways to overcome ________ in every aspect of life."

2."If schools prioritized imagination and critical thinking, students would develop skills to address ________ issues."

3."If society embraced failure as a learning opportunity, individuals would be more willing to ________ and explore new ideas."

Task №2: Paraphrasing Challenge


Based on the text, choose the correct answer:

Listen to the text and paraphrase the following ideas in your own words:
1. "If schools prioritized imagination and critical thinking, students would develop skills to address real-world issues."
2. "If employers encouraged risk-taking and brainstorming, teams would find unique solutions to business challenges."
3. "If society embraced failure as a learning opportunity, individuals would be more willing to experiment and explore new ideas."

Task №3:Creative Brainstorming Activity:


After listening to the text, brainstorm creative ways to solve one of the following real-world problems. Use examples or ideas inspired by the text to help guide your thinking.

Choose one problem to focus on:
• Reducing plastic waste.
• Improving mental health awareness in schools.
•Making workplaces more inclusive and innovative.
