Lesson 13

Reading

Task №1: Inference Questions


•Why had the team been training before starting the expedition?

• What challenges did the team face during their journey?

• Why did Grant remain determined even after the storm?

Task №2: Analyze the Context


Explain the significance of the carved stone in the story.

Task №3:Critical Thinking - Evaluate Perspectives:


Do you think the team was adequately prepared for the expedition? Why or why not?

Writing

Task №1:Essay


Write an essay about a time when you faced multiple challenges while working toward a goal. Use Past Perfect Continuous at least five times.

Task №2: Reflection


Imagine you are Professor Grant. Write a journal entry about the day of the storm and your emotions during the setbacks.

Task №3: Creative Task


Create an alternative ending for the story where the team does not find the ancient city. Explain what happens instead.

Speaking

Task №1: Discussion


Discuss with a partner how the team's preparation helped them during the expedition. Would you have done anything differently?

Task №2: Presentation


Pretend you are Professor Grant. Present the discovery of the ancient city to a group of scientists.

Task №3: Role-Play


One student is Grant, and the other is a journalist. Conduct an interview about the challenges and discoveries of the expedition.

Listening

Task №1:Comprehension Questions


Answer the following questions based on your understanding of the main ideas and examples.

1.What are some common problems faced by archaeologists in the field?

2.How do they overcome environmental obstacles?

3.What skills are necessary for a successful expedition?

Task №2: Problem-Solving


Based on the audio, propose a strategy for how Grant’s team could improve their methods for future expeditions.

Task №3:Comparison Task


Compare the challenges mentioned in the audio to those faced by Grant’s team in the story. Which were more difficult, and why?
