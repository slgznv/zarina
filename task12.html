Lesson 12

Reading

Task №1:


Find all the superlatives in the text and explain their function in the sentences.

Task №2:


Match the complex words to their synonyms or definitions. For example, "vibrant" = energetic.

Task №3:


Based on the text, list three advantages and disadvantages of living in large cities.

Writing

Task №1:


Write a paragraph about your ideal city, using at least five superlatives from the text.

Task №2:


Compare two cities you know well, highlighting their most and least attractive qualities.

Task №3:


Rewrite the conclusion of the text, presenting your opinion about the most livable city.

Speaking

Task №1:


Discuss in pairs: Would you prefer to live in the busiest or the greenest city? Why?

Task №2:


Give a short presentation about a city in your country, using at least five superlatives.

Task №3: Debate


Is affordability the most important factor when choosing a city to live in?

Listening

Task №1:Key Phrase Gap-Fill


Listen to the video and complete the sentences with the missing words or phrases.

•"Beliefs are the _______ for success or failure."

•"People with a growth mindset view _______ as an opportunity to learn."

• "To change your mindset, you need to change the way you think about _______."

Task №2: Note-Taking and Summarizing


Watch the video and take notes on the main points. Focus on:

•Definitions of fixed mindset and growth mindset.

•Examples or stories used to explain these concepts.

• Tips for developing a growth mindset.

Task №3: Listening for Cause and Effect


While watching the video, identify examples of cause and effect relationships. Write down phrases where the speaker explains how a belief impacts success.

•What causes someone to have a fixed mindset?

•How does a growth mindset lead to better outcomes?

• What happens when people view failure as a learning opportunity?
