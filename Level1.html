Lesson 1

Grammar: Mixed Conditionals

Mixed Conditionals are a combination of two different conditional types, typically used to talk about hypothetical situations where the time of the condition (the "if" clause) and the result (the main clause) are different. They often involve mixing the second and third conditional structures.

Two common types of mixed conditionals:

1. Past condition, present result

This is used when an event in the past influences or affects the present.

- Structure:

If + past perfect (had + V3), would + base verb.

- Example:

If I had studied harder, I would have a better job now.

2. Present condition, past result

This is used to express how a current situation or state could have changed a past outcome.

- Structure:

If + past simple (second conditional), would have + past participle (third conditional).

- Example:

If I were more organized, I would have finished the project on time.

Key Point:

The clauses mix time frames to show a past event affecting the present or a present condition affecting the past.

Text


If Claire had studied harder in college, she would have secured a better job by now. She often thinks about how different her life would be if she hadn't skipped so many classes. Her friends who were more diligent in their studies are now working in successful companies, and Claire sometimes feels regret. If she had realized the importance of education back then, she wouldn’t be dealing with financial difficulties today. Instead, she would be enjoying a stable career and higher income.

However, Claire understands that it’s not too late to make changes. If she were more disciplined now, she could still improve her skills and find a better job in the future. She has already started taking online courses, and if she continues with this dedication, she believes she will have a chance to advance in her career. Claire also thinks that if she had saved more money earlier, she wouldn't be struggling to pay for these courses now. But she remains optimistic, knowing that if she keeps working hard, she will succeed eventually.

Moreover, Claire reflects on her personal life and relationships. If she hadn't been so focused on short-term pleasures, she would have formed stronger connections with her family and friends. Now, she wishes she had spent more time with them when she had the chance. If she had balanced her time better in the past, she wouldn't feel so distant from the people she cares about. Still, Claire knows that if she makes an effort to reconnect with them, she will be able to rebuild those relationships.

In terms of health, Claire also regrets her choices. If she had taken care of her body and mind, she wouldn't be experiencing such high levels of stress today. She used to neglect regular exercise and a balanced diet, thinking it wasn’t important. Now, she sees that if she had maintained a healthier lifestyle, she would have more energy and mental clarity. Claire realizes that if she starts focusing on her well-being now, she will see improvements in the long term.

New words:

1. Secured - обеспечила бы

2. Skipped - пропускала

3. Diligent - прилежные

4. Realized - осознала

5. Dealing - справляется

6. Disciplined - дисциплинированная

7. Advance - продвинуться

8. Focused - сосредоточена

9. Experiencing - испытывает

10. Clarity - ясность

READING

Task №1 True or False: Determine whether the statements are true or false based on the text.


- Claire regrets not studying harder in college.

- Claire has already saved enough money for her online courses.

- If Claire had focused more on her health, she would have more energy now.

Task №2 Comprehension Questions:


- Why does Claire feel regret about her education and career choices?

- What does Claire think she could do now to improve her situation?

- How does Claire plan to rebuild her personal relationships?

Task №3 Gap Fill: Fill in the blanks using words from the text.


- Claire thinks she ___ (could/can) still improve her skills if she were more ___ (organized/disciplined).

- If she had ___ (saved/earned) more money earlier, she wouldn’t be ___ (enjoying/struggling) with financial difficulties now.

WRITING

Task №1: Short Essay (100-150 words)

Write about a time when you wished you had done something differently in the past. What impact did it have on your present situation?

Task №2: Email to a Friend

Write an email to a friend, telling them about Claire’s story. Include her regrets, what she’s doing to improve, and give your own advice on how she can succeed in the future.

Task №3: Sentence Transformation


Rewrite these sentences using conditionals:

- Claire didn't save money, so she’s struggling now.

- Claire regrets not taking care of her health.

- If Claire works hard now, she will have a better career.

SPEAKING

Task №1: Discussion Questions


- What would you have done differently if you were in Claire’s situation?

- How important is planning and discipline in achieving career success, in your opinion?

- How do you balance short-term and long-term goals in your own life?

Task №2: Role Play

Imagine you're Claire talking to a career advisor. Discuss what you regret about your past decisions and what steps you can take now to improve your situation.

Task №3: Presentation

Prepare a short presentation about the importance of discipline and focus in both personal and professional life. Use examples from Claire’s story.

LISTENING

Task №1: Listening for Details


Listen to the teacher reading the text and answer these questions:

- How does Claire feel about her financial situation?

- What did Claire neglect that caused her to feel stressed today?

Task №2: Dictation

The teacher will read aloud parts of the text. Write down what you hear, focusing on the correct use of conditionals and vocabulary.

Task №3: Listen and Match


Listen to the audio (the teacher summarizing the text) and match the actions to their consequences. For example:

- If Claire had studied harder → She would have a better job now.

- If Claire had saved more money → ____.
