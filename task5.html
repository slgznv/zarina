Lesson 5

Reading

Task №1:Analyze the Main Challenges


Write an essay analyzing the key environmental challenges mentioned in the text. How do different sectors like energy production, agriculture, and consumers contribute to environmental damage, and what solutions are proposed?

Task №2:Evaluate the Role of Governments and Organizations


2. Discuss how governments and large corporations are responsible for tackling environmental issues. Use examples from the text to explain what actions they must take to ensure sustainability.

Task №3:Summarize Technological Impacts


Summarize how technological advancements have both contributed to and offered solutions for environmental problems. Use the text as a reference for specific examples of both positive and negative impacts.

Writing

Task №1:Argumentative Essay


Write an argumentative essay on whether governments or individuals bear more responsibility for preventing environmental degradation. Use examples from the text to support your position and provide a counter-argument.

Task №2:Case Study on Agricultural Impact


Research how current agricultural practices contribute to greenhouse gas emissions. Compare your findings with the information from the text, and write a report on what measures *should* be taken to reduce the environmental impact of farming.

Task №3:Narrative Story:


Write a fictional story where the main character is part of an organization trying to combat climate change. Use at least five of the bolded vocabulary words to describe the obstacles they face and how they *must* navigate the complexities of environmental sustainability.

Speaking

Task №1:Debate on Renewable Energy


In pairs or small groups, discuss whether renewable energy sources alone are enough to solve the world’s energy problems. One group argues in favor of renewable energy, while the other explains why it may not be sufficient without additional solutions.

Task №2: Discussion on Consumer Responsibility


Organize a group discussion on the role of individual consumers in environmental sustainability. Use examples from the text to argue whether small lifestyle changes *can* make a significant impact on the environment.

Task №3:Presentation on Global Cooperation:


3. Prepare a presentation on the importance of international cooperation in combating climate change. Use the text to highlight why different sectors (energy, agriculture, consumers) need to work together globally to achieve meaningful change.

Listening

Task №1:Podcast Reflection


Listen to a podcast about renewable energy innovations and compare the ideas presented with the arguments in the text. Write a short reflection on whether the podcast supports or contradicts the text’s views on sustainable energy.

Task №2:Watch the video carefully and answer the following questions.


What are the main types of pollution mentioned in the video?

How does pollution affect animals and plants, according to the video?

What are some ways people can reduce pollution?

What visual examples does the cartoon show to illustrate the consequences of pollution?

What message does the video convey about individual responsibility in protecting the environment?

Task №3: Expert Interview Analysis


Listen to an interview with a climate expert discussing global warming and compare it with the concerns raised in the text. Write an analysis of how the expert’s views align with or differ from the text's solutions for tackling climate change.
