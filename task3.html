Lesson 3

Reading

Task №1:


Analyze the relationship between ecosystems in the text. How does the text explain the interconnectedness of forests, oceans, and the atmosphere? Provide evidence from the text to support your answer.

Task №2:


Summarize the section that discusses deforestation and its impact on biodiversity. Use 4-5 new words to rewrite this part of the text in your own words.

Task №3:


What solutions does the author propose to address the problems of pollution and environmental degradation? Explain these solutions and give your opinion on their effectiveness.

Writing

Task №1:


Write an essay discussing the impact of human activity on fragile ecosystems. Use at least 6 of the new vocabulary words to provide specific examples and propose solutions.

Task №2:


Imagine you are a scientist working on a project to restore a damaged ecosystem. Write a report explaining the steps needed for reforestation and the challenges you expect to face during the process.

Task №3:


Create a fictional story where Earth has become a much more hostile place due to climate change. Describe how society has had to adapt to severe weather conditions, and use 4 new words to explain the changes made to survive.

Speaking

Task №1:


Imagine you're presenting a speech at an environmental summit. Explain why *reforestation* is critical to preserving biodiversity and how governments can support this effort. Include at least 5 new vocabulary words in your speech.

Task №2:


2. Have a conversation with a partner where you discuss the deterioration of the oceans. One person argues that stricter laws on pollution are the solution, while the other believes individual actions, like reducing plastic use, are more effective.

Task №3:


You are an environmental activist convincing a local community to switch to renewable energy sources. Create a dialogue using at least 3 new words to persuade them that renewable energy is a better option than fossil fuels.

Listening

Task №1:


Listen to a podcast on climate change. Afterward, summarize the main points and compare them with the ideas presented in the text. Discuss how both sources emphasize the importance of reducing our carbon footprint.

Task №2:


In pairs, listen to a documentary about the extinction of species. One partner summarizes the key points about biodiversity loss, and the other uses 3 of the new words to expand on how deforestation contributes to the problem.

Task №3:


Listen to an audio clip or video about sustainable living practices. Afterward, create a list of the actions mentioned in the audio and discuss with a partner which of them could have the greatest impact if implemented globally.
